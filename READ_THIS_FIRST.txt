NEUROMERA - WHAT WAS WRONG & WHAT'S FIXED
==========================================

Hi! I reviewed your notebook and found why predictions were always wrong
even though accuracy looked good. Here's the short version:


WHAT WAS BROKEN (3 things)
---------------------------

1. STOPWORD REMOVAL WAS KILLING YOUR MODEL
   You were removing common words like "not", "no", "never" before
   feeding text to BERT. So "I am NOT happy" became just "happy" and
   got classified as joy. This is fine for SVM/TF-IDF models, but BERT
   needs the full sentence to understand meaning. This is why switching
   models or datasets didn't help - the bug was in the preprocessing,
   not the model.

2. THE TRAINING STEP WAS MISSING
   The notebook had TrainingArguments and Dataset set up, but never
   actually called trainer.train(). The Trainer object was never
   created. So the model was never trained in the notebook.

3. PREDICTION USED DIFFERENT PREPROCESSING THAN TRAINING
   Training cleaned text one way, but the predict function cleaned it
   a different way (different max_length, different text cleaning).
   The model saw different input at prediction time than what it
   learned from.


WHAT'S FIXED (NeuroMera_Fixed.ipynb)
--------------------------------------

- Raw text goes directly to BERT (no stopword removal)
- Training actually runs (Trainer + trainer.train() included)
- Prediction uses the exact same processing as training
- Label mapping (anger/fear/joy/love/sadness/surprise) is saved
  inside the model, so it works after save/load without needing
  the original CSV


HOW TO RUN IT
--------------

1. Open NeuroMera_Fixed.ipynb in Google Colab

2. Enable GPU:  Runtime > Change runtime type > T4 GPU

3. In Step 1, update the CSV_PATH to point to your dataset on
   Google Drive. Your CSV needs exactly 2 columns:

      text,label
      "i feel so happy today",joy
      "this makes me angry",anger
      ...

   Labels must be one of: sadness, love, anger, joy, fear, surprise

4. Click:  Runtime > Run all

5. Wait ~20 minutes for training to finish (Step 4)

6. Model gets saved to Google Drive automatically (Step 6)

7. Next time you just want to predict without retraining,
   skip to Step 7 and run from there


That's it. The notebook is designed to run top-to-bottom with no
extra steps needed.
